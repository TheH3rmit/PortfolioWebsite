Maze Dive

Maze Dive is a low-poly, 3D first-person shooter (FPS) roguelike game developed in Unity as a group project by a team of three. It was created as part of a university coursework submission for academic credit. The game features procedurally generated maps built from a set of pre-designed room presets, offering unique gameplay experiences with each playthrough.

Key Features and Mechanics:

  1. Shooting Mechanic
    • Players can shoot and aim with precision using a crosshair.
    • Mouse controls are used for shooting and activating weapons.
    • Players must manage ammunition effectively, employing tactical reloading during combat.
  2. Equipment System
    • Players can carry two weapons at a time, switch freely between them, and manage unique ammunition for each weapon type.
  3. Combat and Enemy Defeat
    • Players use shooting mechanics and available weapons to defeat enemies encountered in rooms.
  4. Health Management
    • The game includes a health system that challenges players to fight strategically and manage their health during encounters.
  5. Character Death
    • If the player's health reaches zero, the game ends, transitioning them to the end menu.
  6. Enemy Artificial Intelligence (AI)
    • Enemies spawn dynamically with rooms and operate on a Finite-State Machine (FSM) model with seven states.
    • AI behaviors include seeking cover, shooting, reloading, and chasing the player.
    • Enemies adapt to the player's actions, creating dynamic and engaging combat scenarios.
    • Upon death, enemies transition into ragdolls and disappear after 10 seconds to optimize performance.
  7. Room Generation
    • Maps are procedurally generated using pre-made room presets for variety.
    • Rooms contain dynamic enemy spawns and environmental layouts, encouraging exploration and replayability.
  8. Sound Design
    • Includes unique weapon sounds, enhancing the immersive combat experience.

Development and Design: The game emphasizes a minimalist aesthetic with a low-poly art style, balancing performance and visual appeal. Created as part of university coursework, this project demonstrates strong teamwork, creativity, and technical execution, providing a polished and engaging roguelike FPS experience.
